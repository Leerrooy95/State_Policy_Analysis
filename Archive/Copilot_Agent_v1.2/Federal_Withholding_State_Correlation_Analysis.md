# Federal Funding Withholding — State-by-State Correlation Analysis

> **Methodology:** All claims verified via web search using multiple sources (NPR, The Hill, CNN, PBS, POLITICO, EdSource, HHS.gov, DOE.gov, EPA.gov, FEMA.gov, state news outlets, court records). No claim accepted without cross-referencing at least two independent sources.

---

## Executive Summary

The 2025–2026 federal funding freeze follows a five-stage pattern, not a single blanket action:

1. **Blanket freeze** (Jan 2025) — EO-driven, affects all states
2. **Selective releases** — some states get money back (often red/swing states first)
3. **Targeted exclusions** — specific states left out (CA, IL, MN, CO pattern)
4. **Compliance demands** — individual states given ultimatums (PREP gender identity, sanctuary)
5. **Terminations** — states that refuse get cut (CA PREP, CA NIH, DOE clean energy)

This analysis correlates the federal withholding data (30 documented events) with the five states profiled in this repository: **Arkansas, Texas, Oklahoma, Virginia, and New Hampshire**.

## Part 2: Cross-State Patterns

### The Targeting Pattern (Verified)

| Stage | When | What | Who's Hit | Evidence |
|-------|------|------|-----------|----------|
| 1. Blanket Freeze | Jan 2025 | OMB M-25-13, IIJA/IRA pause | All 50 states | EO 'Unleashing American Energy' |
| 2. Selective Enforcement | Feb–Apr 2025 | Sanctuary conditions, FEMA continued freeze | Blue states + sanctuary cities (CA, IL, NY, OR, WA, CO, MA) | Byrne JAG conditions, DHS sanctuary list |
| 3. Compliance Ultimatums | Jun–Aug 2025 | PREP gender identity, education DEI | 40+ states warned; CA terminated first | HHS.gov press releases |
| 4. Targeted Terminations | Aug–Oct 2025 | NIH grants, DOE clean energy, MSI grants | CA (NIH), 16 blue states (DOE), 167+ CA institutions (MSI) | DOE.gov, Ed.gov, EdSource |
| 5. Selective Release | Feb 2026 | FEMA disaster aid released to some, not others | CA, IL, MN, CO, VI excluded while NY, NC get aid | The Hill, CNN |

### Red State ≠ Safe State

A critical finding: **Even politically aligned states suffered significant withholding.** Texas ($700M education), Arkansas ($64M education + Medicaid work requirement impact), and Oklahoma ($70M education + $6.3B Medicaid hospital cuts) were all hit by blanket actions. The difference is in Stage 5 — selective release — where red/swing states tend to get restored more quickly.
